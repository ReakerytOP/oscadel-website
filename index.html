OSCADEL

Smart Assistive Glasses for the Visually Impaired

About the Project

OSCADEL is an assistive technology project designed to help visually impaired users safely navigate their surroundings. The system uses ultrasonic sensors, audio feedback, and microcontroller-based processing to detect obstacles in real time.

How It Works

Obstacle Detection

Ultrasonic sensors continuously scan the front, left, and right directions to detect nearby objects.

Processing

An Arduino Nano processes distance data and determines the direction of the obstacle.

Audio Feedback

A buzzer or voice module alerts the user with different sounds or spoken directions.

Hardware Components

  • Arduino Nano
  • 3× Ultrasonic Sensors (HC-SR04)
  • Buzzer / DFPlayer Mini (voice output)
  • Battery-powered supply
  • Jumper wires and frame-mounted glasses

Why OSCADEL?

  • Hands-free navigation assistance
  • Directional obstacle awareness
  • Low-cost and portable design
  • Easy to upgrade and customize

Our Journey

The OSCADEL project began as a simple idea: to make navigation safer and more independent for visually impaired individuals. What started as a basic obstacle-detection concept gradually evolved into a fully functional assistive glasses system.

Idea & Research

We studied the challenges faced by visually impaired people and explored existing assistive technologies to identify gaps and improvements.

Design & Prototyping

Using Arduino Nano, ultrasonic sensors, and audio feedback, we built early prototypes and tested different sensor placements on glasses.

Testing & Improvements

Multiple iterations helped us improve accuracy, reduce false detections, and make the system more comfortable and reliable.

Final Showcase

The refined OSCADEL system is now ready for demonstrations and events, showcasing practical, low-cost assistive innovation.

Community Feedback

User trials and community input helped us prioritize features that really matter in day-to-day mobility and independence.

Next Steps

We plan to refine the design for production, improve audio guidance, and partner with local organizations to test the device in real-world environments.
